## Dots and Boxes Game Rules

Dots and Boxes is a classic pencil-and-paper game where players take turns drawing lines to complete boxes.

### 1. Setup & Preliminaries
- **Players:** 2 (Player 0 and Player 1)
- **Board:** 3×3 grid of dots (creates a 2×2 grid of potential boxes)
- **Lines:** Players draw horizontal or vertical lines between adjacent dots
- **Player 0 goes first**

### 2. Turn Structure
On your turn:
1. Draw exactly one line between two adjacent dots (horizontally or vertically)
2. If your line completes one or more boxes, you claim those boxes AND take another turn
3. If your line doesn't complete any box, your turn ends

### 3. Completing Boxes
- A box is completed when all four of its edges are drawn
- The player who draws the fourth edge claims the box
- Completing a box earns you an extra turn (you can chain multiple completions)

### 4. Winning
- Game ends when all lines are drawn (all boxes completed)
- Player with more boxes wins (+1.0, opponent -1.0)
- If equal boxes, it's a draw (both get 0.0)

### 5. Board Layout
Dots are arranged in a grid. For a 3×3 dot grid:
```
0---1---2
|   |   |
3---4---5
|   |   |
6---7---8
```
This creates 4 potential boxes (2×2).

### 6. Move Notation
Lines are specified as "h_R_C" (horizontal) or "v_R_C" (vertical):
- **h_R_C:** Horizontal line from dot (R,C) to dot (R,C+1)
- **v_R_C:** Vertical line from dot (R,C) to dot (R+1,C)

For a 3×3 dot grid:
- Horizontal lines: h_0_0, h_0_1, h_1_0, h_1_1, h_2_0, h_2_1
- Vertical lines: v_0_0, v_0_1, v_0_2, v_1_0, v_1_1, v_1_2

### Example Trajectory

Step 0 (Player 0): The game begins on a grid of dots. It is Player 0's turn. From the available empty spaces between adjacent dots, Player 0 chooses to draw a horizontal line in the top-left corner of the grid.
Step 1 (Player 1): It is Player 1's turn. Player 1 chooses to draw another horizontal line adjacent to the first one.
...
Step N (Player 0): It is Player 0's turn. Player 0 draws a vertical line that closes off the fourth and final side of a 1x1 square on the grid. Player 0 claims this completed box and scores a point.
Step N+1 (Player 0 - Extra Turn): Because Player 0 successfully completed a box on their previous action, the rules dictate that they are granted an immediate extra turn. Player 0 goes again, drawing another horizontal line elsewhere on the grid.
...
Step M (Terminal): The final possible line is drawn on the board, leaving no more empty spaces between dots. The game ends immediately. Player 0 has claimed a total of 3 boxes, while Player 1 has claimed 1 box. Having secured the majority of the boxes, 
Player 0 receives a full win (+1.0), and Player 1 receives a loss (-1.0). No further actions can be taken.

## Action String Specification
Player actions:
  - Horizontal line: format "h_<row>_<col>" where row,col are 0-indexed
    e.g. "h_0_0", "h_2_1"
  - Vertical line: format "v_<row>_<col>"
    e.g. "v_0_0", "v_1_2"
