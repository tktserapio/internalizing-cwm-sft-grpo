## Game of Y Rules

Y is a connection game for two players, invented by Craige Schensted and Charles Titus. Players take turns placing stones on a triangular board, trying to connect all three sides.

### 1. Setup
- **Players:** 2 (Black and White)
- **Board:** A triangular grid of hexagonal cells. Common sizes are 4-12 cells per edge. A size-4 board has 10 cells total.
- **Starting position:** The board begins empty.
- **First player:** Black plays first.

### 2. Board Structure
The board is triangular with three distinct sides (edges). Corner cells touch two sides. For a size-4 board:
```
       A
      / \
     1   2
    / \ / \
   3   4   5
  / \ / \ / \
 6   7   8   9
  \   \   \   \
   B ------- C
```
- Side A-B: cells 1, 3, 6
- Side A-C: cells 2, 5, 9
- Side B-C: cells 6, 7, 8, 9
- Corner cells: 6 (touches A-B and B-C), 9 (touches A-C and B-C), 1 and 2 share the A corner

### 3. Gameplay
- Players alternate turns, placing one stone of their color on any empty cell.
- Stones are never moved or removed once placed.
- There is no capturing.

### 4. Winning Condition
A player wins by forming a connected group of their stones that touches all three sides of the triangle.

- "Connected" means stones are adjacent (sharing an edge on the hex grid).
- A corner cell counts as touching both of its adjacent sides.
- The game cannot end in a draw - when the board fills, exactly one player will have a winning connection.

### 5. Move Notation
Moves are denoted by the cell identifier (letter+number or just number depending on notation system):
- Example: "7" places a stone on cell 7
- Example: "B3" places a stone on cell B3

### 6. Observation Space
- **Public information:** The entire board state is visible to both players.
- **Private information:** None. Y is a perfect information game.

### 7. Strategy Notes
- Corner cells are valuable since they touch two sides.
- The center is strategically important for connecting distant edges.
- Like Hex, Y favors the first player, so a swap rule is sometimes used.

## Action String Specification
Player actions: format "<row>,<col>" where row and col are 0-indexed integers
  - e.g. "0,0", "4,9", "9,4"
  - Both players use the same format (player identity tracked via turn order)
