## Liar's Dice Game Rules

Liar's Dice is a classic dice game of bluffing and deception. It involves players making bids on the number of dice showing a certain face, with the option to challenge the previous bid if they believe it to be false. Below are the comprehensive rules for playing Liar's Dice.

### 1. Setup & Preliminaries
- **Players:** 2 (Player 1 (P1), Player 2 (P2)).
- **Dice:** Each player starts with 5 six-sided dice.
- **Cups:** Each player has a cup to conceal their dice.
- **Initial Roll:** At the beginning of each round, all players roll their dice under their cups, keeping the result hidden from other players.

### 2. Game Structure
- **Rounds:** The game is played in rounds, with each round consisting of a series of bids and challenges.
- **Starting Player:** The first player to bid is chosen randomly or by agreement.

### 3. Bidding
- **Bid Format:** A bid is made in the format "X-Y" where X is the quantity of dice and Y is the face value (e.g., "3-4" means three dice showing a face value of four).
- **Valid Bids:** A bid must either:
  - Increase the quantity (X) of the previous bid.
  - If the quantity remains the same, increase the face value (Y).
- **Wild Ones:** Ones (1s) are wild and count as any face value when determining the validity of a bid. However, a bid specifically on ones (e.g., "2-1") is not wild.

### 4. Challenging
- **Challenge Action:** A player can challenge the previous bid if they believe it to be false.
- **Challenge Resolution:** When a challenge is made:
  - All players reveal their dice.
  - Count the total number of dice showing the face value of the last bid, including ones if they are wild.
  - If the bid is valid (i.e., the total is equal to or greater than the bid), the challenger loses one die.
  - If the bid is invalid, the player who made the bid loses one die.

### 5. Ending a Round
- **Round Termination:** A round ends when a challenge is resolved.
- **Next Round:** The player who lost a die starts the next round.

### 6. Winning the Game
- **Elimination:** A player is eliminated when they lose all their dice.
- **Victory:** The last player remaining with dice is the winner.

### 7. Observation Space
- **Public Information:** The current bid and the number of dice each player has.
- **Private Information:** Each player's dice results.

### 8. Move Notation
- **Bid:** "X-Y" where X is the quantity and Y is the face value.
- **Challenge:** "C" to indicate a challenge of the previous bid.

### Example Trajectory
Step 0 (Chance/Setup): The game begins. The system secretly rolls five dice for Player 0. The result is a 1, 2, 3, 4, and 5. Player 0 can only see their own dice; they remain strictly hidden under the cup from Player 1.
Step 1 (Chance/Setup): The system secretly rolls five dice for Player 1. The result is a 2, 3, 4, 5, and 6. These are completely hidden from Player 0.
Step 2 (Player 0): It is Player 0's turn to make the opening bid. Guessing the collective pool of dice across all players, Player 0 bids that there are at least "two 3s" currently in play.
Step 3 (Player 1): It is Player 1's turn. Player 1 must now either raise the bid (by increasing the quantity of dice or the face value) or challenge the previous bid if they believe it is a lie. Player 1 chooses to challenge Player 0's bid of "two 3s".
Step 4 (Terminal): The challenge immediately halts the betting phase and forces a showdown. All dice are revealed. Player 0 reveals [1, 2, 3, 4, 5] and Player 1 reveals [2, 3, 4, 5, 6]. Following standard rules, '1's act as wildcards and count toward the called face value. The board contains one '1' and two '3's, making a total of three 3s. Because the actual count (3) meets or exceeds Player 0's bid (2), the bid was valid. Player 1 challenged incorrectly and is penalized by losing a die. The round ends immediately. Player 0 is safe and receives a payoff of 0.0, while the penalized Player 1 receives a payoff of -1.0. No further actions can be taken.

This example demonstrates a simple round where P1 makes a bid, P2 challenges, and the dice are revealed to resolve the challenge.

## Action String Specification
Player actions:
  - Bid: format "bid:<quantity>:<face>" e.g. "bid:2:3", "bid:4:6"
  - Challenge: "liar"
Chance actions: format "roll:<value>" where value ∈ {1,2,3,4,5,6}
  - e.g. "roll:3", "roll:6"
