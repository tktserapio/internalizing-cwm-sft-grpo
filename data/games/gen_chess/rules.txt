## Generalized Chess (5x5 Gardner Minichess) Rules

This is Gardner's Minichess, a 5×5 variant of chess that preserves the essential character of chess while being more tractable for analysis.

### 1. Setup & Preliminaries
- **Players:** 2 (Player 0 plays White, Player 1 plays Black)
- **Board:** 5×5 grid
- **White plays first**

### 2. Initial Position
```
5: r n b q k   (Black)
4: p p p p p
3: . . . . .
2: P P P P P
1: R N B Q K   (White)
   a b c d e
```

### 3. Pieces and Movement
Same as standard chess:
- **King (K):** One square any direction
- **Queen (Q):** Any number of squares horizontally, vertically, or diagonally
- **Rook (R):** Any number of squares horizontally or vertically
- **Bishop (B):** Any number of squares diagonally
- **Knight (N):** L-shape (2+1), can jump
- **Pawn (P):** Forward one (or two from start), captures diagonally

### 4. Special Rules
- **Castling:** Not available (only one rook, no space)
- **En Passant:** Available when a pawn moves two squares
- **Promotion:** Pawn reaching the opposite rank promotes (Queen, Rook, Bishop, or Knight)

### 5. Check and Checkmate
- **Check:** King under attack
- **Checkmate:** King in check with no legal escape (game ends)
- **Stalemate:** No legal moves but not in check (draw)

### 6. Draw Conditions
- Stalemate
- 50-move rule (50 moves without pawn move or capture)
- Insufficient material

### 7. Move Notation
Standard algebraic with underscores:
- **Piece_from_to:** e.g., "N_b1_c3"
- **Pawn:** "P_e2_e4"
- **Promotion:** "P_e4_e5_Q"
- **Capture:** Same notation (captured piece implicit)

### 8. Board Coordinates
- Files: a-e (columns, left to right)
- Ranks: 1-5 (rows, bottom to top from White's view)
- White starts on ranks 1-2, Black on ranks 4-5

### Example Trajectory

Step 0: current_player=0, legal_actions=["P_a2_a3", "P_a2_a4", ..., "N_b1_c3", "N_b1_a3"], rewards=[0.0, 0.0]
  -> Action: "P_e2_e4"  # White advances king pawn
Step 1: current_player=1, legal_actions=["P_a4_a3", ..., "N_b5_c3"], rewards=[0.0, 0.0]
  -> Action: "P_e4_e3"  # Black responds
Step 2: current_player=0, legal_actions=[...], rewards=[0.0, 0.0]
  -> Action: "Q_d1_e2"  # White develops queen
...
Step N (TERMINAL): current_player=-4, legal_actions=[], rewards=[1.0, -1.0]
  # Checkmate, White wins
