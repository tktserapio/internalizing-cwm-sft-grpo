## Kuhn Poker Game Rules

Kuhn Poker is a simplified two-player poker variant that emphasizes strategic betting and bluffing. It uses a minimal deck of cards and has straightforward betting rules, making it an excellent choice for AI research. Below are the comprehensive rules for playing Kuhn Poker.

### 1. Setup & Preliminaries
- **Players:** 2 (Player 1 (P1) and Player 2 (P2)).
- **Deck:** 3 cards (King (K), Queen (Q), Jack (J)). Each card is unique.
- **Blinds:** There are no blinds in Kuhn Poker.
- **The Deal:** Each player is dealt one private card face down. The cards are drawn randomly from the deck.

### 2. Core Betting Rules
- **Betting Actions:** Players can perform the following actions during their turn:
  - **Fold (F):** Forfeit the hand and the pot.
  - **Call (C):** Match the current bet.
  - **Raise (R):** Increase the current bet by a fixed amount.
  
- **Raise Sizing:** The amount to raise is fixed at **1 unit** for both rounds.
- **Total Betting Cap:** The total betting cap for each round is a maximum of **one raise** per player.

### 3. Round 1: Pre-Flop Betting
This round occurs before any public cards are revealed.
- **P1's First Action:** P1 acts first.
  - **Fold (F):** P1 forfeits the hand. P2 wins the pot.
  - **Call (C):** P1 matches the bet of 0 units (no initial bet).
  - **Raise (R):** P1 raises the bet by 1 unit (total bet becomes 1 unit).

- **P2's Action:**
  - If P1 **folded**, the round ends, and P2 wins the pot.
  - If P1 **called**, P2 can:
    - **Fold (F):** Forfeit the hand and lose the pot.
    - **Call (C):** Match the bet of 0 units (no additional cost).
    - **Raise (R):** Raise the bet by 1 unit (total bet becomes 1 unit).
  - If P1 **raised**, P2 can:
    - **Fold (F):** Forfeit the hand and lose the pot.
    - **Call (C):** Match the bet of 1 unit (total bet becomes 1 unit).

### 4. Round 2: Showdown
After the betting concludes in Round 1, if both players have not folded, a showdown occurs.

- **Showdown:** Each player reveals their private card.
- **Hand Ranking:**
  - **K > Q > J:** The player with the higher card wins.
  
- **Ties:** If both players have the same card (not possible in this variant), the pot is split. However, since there are no duplicate cards, ties cannot occur.

### 5. Winning
A player wins the pot in one of two ways:
- By being the only player remaining after the other player folds.
- By having the higher-ranked card during the showdown.

### 6. Move Notation
- Moves can be represented using the following notation:
  - **Fold** for Fold
  - **Call** for Call (also used as Check when there is no outstanding bet)
  - **Raise** for Raise

### Example Trajectory
Step 0 (Chance/Setup): The game begins. The system randomly and secretly deals one card to each player from a limited deck (e.g., Jack, Queen, King). Player 0 receives the King, and Player 1 receives the Jack. Each player can only see their own private card; the opponent's card remains hidden.
Step 1 (Player 0): It is Player 0's turn to act first. Player 0 chooses to Call (also known as checking in this scenario), passing the action to Player 1 without adding extra chips to the pot.
Step 2 (Player 1): It is Player 1's turn. Despite holding the lowest-ranked card (Jack), Player 1 chooses to Raise, adding an extra chip to the pot and forcing Player 0 to react.
Step 3 (Player 0): It is Player 0's turn again. Facing a raise, Player 0's available options are now strictly limited to Fold (forfeiting the hand) or Call (matching the bet). Holding the highest possible card (King), Player 0 chooses to Call.
Step 4 (Terminal): Because Player 1's raise was called, the betting round concludes and the game proceeds to a showdown. Both players reveal their hidden cards. Player 0 shows the King, and Player 1 shows the Jack. Since a King outranks a Jack, Player 0 wins the hand. The game ends immediately. Player 0 wins the expanded pot for a final payoff of +2.0, and Player 1 receives a final payoff of -2.0. No further actions can be taken.

## Action String Specification
Player actions: ["Fold", "Call", "Raise"]
  - "Fold": forfeit the hand
  - "Call": match the current bet (also used as a zero-cost check when no bet is outstanding)
  - "Raise": increase the bet by 1 unit
Chance actions: format "<P0_card><P1_card>" where card ∈ {J, Q, K}, e.g. "KJ", "QK"
