## Texas Hold'em Poker Game Rules

Texas Hold'em is a popular poker variant that involves strategic betting and card play. It combines private and community cards to form the best possible five-card hand. Below are the comprehensive rules for playing Texas Hold'em Poker.

### 1. Setup & Preliminaries
- **Players:** 2 players.
- **Deck:** Standard 52-card deck without jokers.
- **Blinds (Heads-Up Rules):**
  - **Dealer Button:** The player with the button is the **Small Blind (SB)**.
  - **Opponent:** The other player is the **Big Blind (BB)**.
  - *Note: This is the standard rule for 2-player (Heads-Up) games.*
- **Turn Order:**
  - **Pre-Flop:** The Small Blind (Dealer) acts first.
  - **Post-Flop:** The Big Blind (Non-Dealer) acts first.

### 2. Game Structure
Texas Hold'em consists of four betting rounds: Pre-Flop, Flop, Turn, and River.

#### 2.1. Pre-Flop
- **The Deal:** Each player is dealt two private cards (hole cards) face down.
- **First Betting Round:**
  - Starts with the **Small Blind** (Dealer).
  - **Actions:** 
    - **Fold (F):** Forfeit the hand.
    - **Call (C):** Match the current highest bet (initially the Big Blind).
    - **Raise (R):** Increase the current bet.

#### 2.2. Flop
- **Community Cards:** Three community cards are dealt face up.
- **Second Betting Round:**
  - Starts with the **first active player left of the dealer button** (The Big Blind).
  - **Actions:** Same as Pre-Flop.

#### 2.3. Turn
- **Community Card:** One additional community card is dealt face up.
- **Third Betting Round:**
  - Starts with the first active player left of the dealer button.
  - **Actions:** Same as Pre-Flop.

#### 2.4. River
- **Community Card:** One final community card is dealt face up.
- **Fourth Betting Round:**
  - Starts with the first active player left of the dealer button.
  - **Actions:** Same as Pre-Flop.

### 3. Showdown
- **If more than one player remains after the final betting round:**
  - Players reveal their hole cards.
  - The best five-card hand is determined using any combination of the five community cards and the player's two hole cards.
- **Hand Rankings (from highest to lowest):**
  - **Royal Flush:** A, K, Q, J, 10, all of the same suit.
  - **Straight Flush:** Five consecutive cards of the same suit.
  - **Four of a Kind:** Four cards of the same rank.
  - **Full House:** Three of a kind plus a pair.
  - **Flush:** Five cards of the same suit.
  - **Straight:** Five consecutive cards of different suits.
  - **Three of a Kind:** Three cards of the same rank.
  - **Two Pair:** Two different pairs.
  - **One Pair:** Two cards of the same rank.
  - **High Card:** Highest card when no other hand is made.

### 4. Winning
- A player wins the pot by having the best hand at showdown or by all other players folding.

### 5. Move Notation
- Moves can be represented using the following notation:
  - **F** for Fold
  - **C** for Call
  - **R** for Raise (followed by the amount raised, e.g., R50 for raising 50 units)
  - **K** for Check (if no bet has been made)

### 6. Observation Space
- **Public Information:**
  - Community cards (Flop, Turn, River).
  - Total pot size.
  - Current bet amounts.
  - Actions taken by each player.
- **Private Information:**
  - Each player's hole cards.
