## Havannah Game Rules

Havannah is a strategic board game for two players that involves creating specific patterns on a hexagonal board. The game emphasizes strategic placement and pattern recognition, making it an excellent choice for AI research. Below are the comprehensive rules for playing Havannah.

### 1. Setup & Preliminaries
- **Players:** 2 (Player 1 (P1) and Player 2 (P2)).
- **Board:** A hexagonal grid with a size of 10 hexes per side. The board consists of hexagonal cells.
- **Pieces:** Each player has an unlimited supply of pieces, typically represented by stones of different colors (e.g., black for P1 and white for P2).

### 2. Objective
The objective of Havannah is to be the first player to complete one of the following three winning patterns:
1. **Ring:** A continuous loop of connected stones that encloses one or more hexes.
2. **Bridge:** A continuous chain of stones connecting any two of the six corner hexes of the board.
3. **Fork:** A continuous chain of stones connecting any three of the six corner hexes of the board.

### 3. Game Play
- **Starting Player:** P1 starts the game by placing one of their stones on any unoccupied hex on the board.
- **Turn Sequence:** Players alternate turns, with each player placing one stone on any unoccupied hex during their turn.

### 4. Winning Conditions
- A player wins immediately upon completing any one of the three winning patterns (Ring, Bridge, or Fork) with their stones.
- If the board is completely filled without either player achieving a winning pattern, the game is a draw.

### 5. Move Notation
Moves are represented using a coordinate system based on the hexagonal grid. The board is labeled with letters for columns and numbers for rows, starting from the top-left corner:
- **Columns:** Labeled A to T (for a 10-hex-per-side board).
- **Rows:** Numbered 1 to 19.

A move is denoted by the column letter followed by the row number. For example, placing a stone on the hex in column C and row 5 is represented as "C5".

### 6. Example Trajectory
Step 0 (Player 0): The game begins on the grid. It is Player 0's turn. From the available empty intersections on the board, Player 0 chooses to place a stone at coordinate J10.
Step 1 (Player 1): It is Player 1's turn. Choosing from the remaining empty spaces, Player 1 places a stone at coordinate K10.
Step 2 (Player 0): It is Player 0's turn again. Player 0 places a stone at coordinate I10.
Step 3 (Player 1): Player 1 places a stone at coordinate L10.
Step 4 (Player 0): Player 0 places a stone at coordinate H10. By placing this specific stone, Player 0 successfully forms an unbroken, continuous chain of pieces that completes a bridge connecting the two required corners of the board.
Step 5 (Terminal): Because Player 0 has achieved the connection objective, the game ends immediately. Player 0 receives a full win (+1.0), and Player 1 receives a loss (-1.0). No further actions can be taken.

### 7. Additional Rules
- **Draws:** If the board is completely filled without a winner, the game ends in a draw.
- **Illegal Moves:** Players cannot place stones on occupied hexes. Any attempt to do so is considered an illegal move.

These rules provide a comprehensive framework for understanding and implementing the game of Havannah, suitable for training AI models to play the game effectively.

## Action String Specification
Player actions: format "<col><row>" using letter-number notation on the hexagonal board
  - Column is a capital letter (A, B, C, ...), row is an integer
  - e.g. "A1", "E5", "J10"
  - Both players use the same format (player identity tracked via turn order)
