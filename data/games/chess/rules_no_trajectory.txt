## Chess Game Rules

Chess is a two-player strategy board game played on an 8×8 board. This implementation includes all standard rules except threefold repetition.

### 1. Setup & Preliminaries
- **Players:** 2 (Player 0 plays White, Player 1 plays Black)
- **Board:** 8×8 grid with algebraic notation (a-h columns, 1-8 rows)
- **White plays first**

### 2. Pieces and Movement
Each player starts with 16 pieces:
- **King (K):** Moves one square in any direction
- **Queen (Q):** Moves any number of squares horizontally, vertically, or diagonally
- **Rook (R):** Moves any number of squares horizontally or vertically
- **Bishop (B):** Moves any number of squares diagonally
- **Knight (N):** Moves in an L-shape (2+1 squares); can jump over pieces
- **Pawn (P):** Moves forward one square (or two from starting position); captures diagonally

### 3. Special Moves
- **Castling:** King moves two squares toward a rook; rook moves to other side of king. Requires: neither piece has moved, no pieces between them, king not in check and doesn't pass through check
- **En Passant:** Pawn can capture an adjacent opponent pawn that just moved two squares
- **Promotion:** Pawn reaching the opposite end promotes to Q, R, B, or N

### 4. Check and Checkmate
- **Check:** King is under attack
- **Checkmate:** King is in check with no legal escape moves (game ends, attacker wins)
- **Stalemate:** Player to move has no legal moves but is not in check (draw)

### 5. Draw Conditions
- Stalemate
- 50-move rule (50 moves without pawn move or capture)
- Insufficient material (K vs K, K+B vs K, K+N vs K)

### 6. Move Notation
Standard algebraic notation with underscores:
- **Piece_from_to:** e.g., "N_g1_f3" (Knight from g1 to f3)
- **Pawn_from_to:** e.g., "P_e2_e4" (Pawn from e2 to e4)
- **Castling:** "O-O" (kingside), "O-O-O" (queenside)
- **Promotion:** "P_e7_e8_Q" (pawn promotes to queen)
- **Capture:** Same notation (captured piece is implicit)

### 7. Board Layout
White pieces start on rows 1-2, Black on rows 7-8.
Initial position (White's view from bottom):
```
8: r n b q k b n r  (Black pieces)
7: p p p p p p p p
6: . . . . . . . .
5: . . . . . . . .
4: . . . . . . . .
3: . . . . . . . .
2: P P P P P P P P
1: R N B Q K B N R  (White pieces)
   a b c d e f g h
```

## Action String Specification
Player actions: format "<piece>_<from>_<to>" for regular moves, "<piece>_<from>_<to>_<promo>" for pawn promotion
  - Piece codes: P (pawn), N (knight), B (bishop), R (rook), Q (queen), K (king)
  - Squares use algebraic notation: a1–h8
  - e.g. "P_e2_e4", "N_g1_f3", "B_f1_c4", "P_e7_e8_Q" (promotion)
Castling: "O-O" (kingside), "O-O-O" (queenside)
