## Blackjack Game Rules

Blackjack, also known as 21, is a popular card game played in casinos worldwide. The objective is to have a hand value closer to 21 than the dealer's hand without exceeding 21. Below are the comprehensive rules for playing standard Blackjack.

### 1. Setup & Preliminaries
- **Players:** 1 player (player 0) against a dealer (player 1).
- **Deck:** Standard 52-card deck.
- **Card Values:**
  - Number cards (2-10) are worth their face value.
  - Face cards (Jack, Queen, King) are worth 10.
  - Aces can be worth 1 or 11, whichever is more favorable to the hand.

### 2. Objective
- The goal is to have a hand value as close to 21 as possible without exceeding it (busting).
- Beat the dealer's hand by having a higher total without busting.

### 3. Player IDs
- **-1:** Chance node (deals cards)
- **0:** Player
- **1:** Dealer
- **-4:** Terminal (game over)

### 4. Game Flow & Phases

#### Initial Deal (4 Chance Nodes)
Cards are dealt one at a time via chance nodes. Each chance action has format `deal:<card>` where card is rank+suit (e.g., "deal:Ah", "deal:Ts").

1. **Phase "deal_p1":** Chance deals first card to player
2. **Phase "deal_d1":** Chance deals first card to dealer (upcard)
3. **Phase "deal_p2":** Chance deals second card to player
4. **Phase "deal_d2":** Chance deals second card to dealer (hole card, hidden from player)

After deal_d2, current_player becomes 0 (player) and phase becomes "play".

#### Player Turn (current_player=0, phase="play")
Player can perform the following actions:
- **Hit (H):** Request another card. Sets phase to "p_hit" and current_player to -1 (chance).
- **Stand (S):** Keep current hand. If more hands to play (from split), move to next hand. Otherwise, dealer's turn.
- **Double Down (D):** Double the bet, take exactly one more card (via chance node), then must stand.
- **Split (P):** If first two cards have same rank/value, split into two hands. Requires additional bet.
- **Surrender (R):** Forfeit half the bet and end immediately. Only available with 2 cards.

When player has 21, only "S" (stand) is legal.

#### Player Hit (current_player=-1, phase="p_hit")
Chance node deals a card to player. If player busts (>21), game ends immediately. Otherwise, returns to player turn (phase="play").

#### Dealer Turn (current_player=1)
Dealer must:
- **Hit (H):** If hand value < 17
- **Stand (S):** If hand value >= 17

When dealer hits, phase becomes "d_hit" and current_player becomes -1 (chance).

#### Dealer Hit (current_player=-1, phase="d_hit")
Chance node deals a card to dealer, then returns to dealer turn.

#### Game End
When dealer stands, game resolves and current_player becomes -4.

### 5. Winning Conditions
- **Blackjack:** An Ace and a 10-value card dealt initially pays 1.5:1.
- **Win:** Player's hand value is higher than dealer's without busting.
- **Loss:** Player busts, or dealer's hand value is higher without busting.
- **Push:** Equal hand values, no win or loss.

### 6. Action Notation
- **Chance actions:** `deal:<card>` where card is rank+suit (e.g., "deal:Ah", "deal:7c")
- **Player actions:** H, S, D, P, R
- **Dealer actions:** H, S

### Example Trajectory
Step 0 (Chance): The game begins. A card is dealt from the deck to the player: the 10 of Spades.
Step 1 (Chance): A card is dealt face-up to the dealer: the 7 of Hearts.
Step 2 (Chance): A second card is dealt to the player: the 5 of Diamonds. The player's hand now totals 15.
Step 3 (Chance): A second card is dealt face-down to the dealer (the hole card): the 9 of Clubs.
Step 4 (Player): It is the player's turn to act. From the initial available options (Hit, Stand, Double Down, Surrender, or Split), the player chooses to Hit.
Step 5 (Chance): A card is dealt to the player: the 4 of Hearts. The player's hand now totals 19.
Step 6 (Player): It is the player's turn again. Having already taken a hit, the player's options are now restricted to Hit or Stand. The player chooses to Stand.
Step 7 (Dealer): It is the dealer's turn. The dealer reveals their hidden hole card, showing a total of 16 (7 of Hearts + 9 of Clubs). Following standard rules, the dealer is forced to Hit.
Step 8 (Chance): A card is dealt to the dealer: the King of Diamonds. The dealer's hand totals 26, causing them to bust.
Step 9 (Terminal): Because the dealer busted, the player wins. The game ends immediately. The player receives a full win (+1.0), and the dealer receives a loss (-1.0).

## Action String Specification
Player actions: ["H", "S", "D", "R", "P"]
  - "H": Hit (draw a card)
  - "S": Stand (end turn)
  - "D": Double down
  - "R": Surrender
  - "P": Split (when holding a pair)
Chance actions: format "deal:<rank><suit>" e.g. "deal:Ah", "deal:Tc", "deal:2s"
