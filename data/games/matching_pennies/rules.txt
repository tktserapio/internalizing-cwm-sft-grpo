## Matching Pennies Game Rules

Matching Pennies is a classic two-player zero-sum game from game theory. It is a simultaneous-move game where players choose their actions without knowing what the other player will do.

### 1. Setup & Preliminaries
- **Players:** 2 (Player 0 and Player 1)
- **Actions:** Each player secretly chooses either "heads" or "tails"
- **Simultaneous Play:** Both players reveal their choices at the same time

### 2. Game Flow
1. **CHANCE node** determines the order of secret selection (both players commit without seeing the other's choice)
2. **Player 0** secretly commits to either "heads" or "tails"
3. **Player 1** secretly commits to either "heads" or "tails" (without seeing P0's choice)
4. **Reveal:** Both choices are revealed simultaneously

### 3. Winning Conditions
- **Player 0 wins** if both players choose the same side (both heads or both tails)
- **Player 1 wins** if the players choose different sides

### 4. Payoffs
- Winner receives +1.0, loser receives -1.0
- This is a zero-sum game: payoffs always sum to zero

### 5. Move Notation
- **H** for Heads
- **T** for Tails

### 6. Strategic Notes
- This game has no pure strategy Nash equilibrium
- The mixed strategy Nash equilibrium is for each player to randomize 50-50
- Player 0 (the "matcher") wants outcomes to match
- Player 1 (the "mismatcher") wants outcomes to differ

### Example Trajectory
Step 0 (Player 0): The game begins. It is Player 0's turn to make a secret selection. From the available options of Heads or Tails, Player 0 chooses Heads. This choice is immediately locked in, but it remains completely hidden from Player 1 to simulate simultaneous play.
Step 1 (Player 1): It is Player 1's turn to make their selection. With absolutely no knowledge of what Player 0 has chosen, Player 1 chooses Tails.
Step 2 (Terminal): Both locked-in choices are now revealed simultaneously. Player 0 chose Heads, and Player 1 chose Tails. 
According to the rules, Player 0 wins if the coins match, and Player 1 wins if the coins mismatch. Because the coins show different faces (a mismatch), Player 1 is declared the winner. The game ends immediately. Player 0 receives a loss (-1.0), and Player 1 receives a full win (+1.0). No further actions can be taken.

## Action String Specification
Player actions: ["H", "T"]
  - "H": Heads
  - "T": Tails
