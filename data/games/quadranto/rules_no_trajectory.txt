Quadranto is a partially observable game in which two players try to catch each
other in a 4 by 4 matrix.
The 4 by 4 matrix is divided in 4 quadrants. At the beginning, player 0 is
randomly placed in the top left quadrant and player 1 is randomly placed in the
bottom right quadrant.
During their turn, each player can choose to move in each of the four cardinal
directions, "Left", "Right", "Up", "Down". Or they can choose to "Stay", which
means they remain where they are. When a player moves, if it lands on the same
location where the other player is, it wins and the game ends.
The observation tells the player where it is located and in which *quadrant* the
opponent player is located. Therefore, neither player knows exactly where the
other player is located until the very moment in which one player catches the
other.
If the players perform a total of 20 moves without catching each other, the game
ends in a draw, both players get 0 points. If one catches the other, the winning
player gets +1 points and the losing player gets -1 points.

## Action String Specification
Setup phase actions (placing units):
  - P0 places: format "place_p0:<row>,<col>" e.g. "place_p0:0,0"
  - P1 places: format "place_p1:<row>,<col>" e.g. "place_p1:3,3"
Movement phase actions: ["Up", "Down", "Left", "Right", "Stay"]
