## Pig Dice Game Rules

Pig is a simple jeopardy dice game where players risk accumulated points for the chance to score more.

### 1. Setup & Preliminaries
- **Players:** 2 (Player 0 and Player 1)
- **Equipment:** One 6-sided die
- **Win Condition:** First player to reach 100 points wins
- **Player 0 goes first**

### 2. Turn Structure
On your turn, you repeatedly choose to either:
- **Roll:** Roll the die and see the result
- **Hold:** Bank your turn total and end your turn

### 3. Rolling Rules
- **Roll 2-6:** Add the die value to your turn total. You may roll again or hold.
- **Roll 1:** You lose ALL points accumulated this turn. Your turn ends immediately. (This is called "pigging out")

### 4. Holding
When you hold:
- Your turn total is added to your permanent score
- Your turn ends and play passes to the opponent

### 5. Winning
- The game ends immediately when a player's permanent score reaches or exceeds 100
- That player wins (+1.0 reward, opponent gets -1.0)

### 6. Game Flow
This is a game with chance nodes:
1. Player decides: "roll" or "hold"
2. If "roll", a CHANCE node determines the die outcome (1-6, each with probability 1/6)
3. Based on outcome, either turn continues or ends

### 7. Move Notation
- **roll:** Choose to roll the die
- **hold:** Bank points and end turn
- **CHANCE actions:** "1", "2", "3", "4", "5", "6" (die outcomes)

### Example Trajectory

Step 0 (Player 0): The game begins. It is Player 0's turn. Because they just started their turn and have zero temporary points accumulated, they cannot hold. Their only legal option is to roll the die. Player 0 chooses to roll.
Step 1 (Chance): The system rolls a standard six-sided die. The result is a 5.
Step 2 (Player 0): The 5 is added to Player 0's temporary point total for this turn. Because they did not roll a 1 (which would forfeit all temporary points and end their turn), they now have a choice: risk their temporary points by rolling again, or safely hold. Player 0 chooses to risk it and roll again.
Step 3 (Chance): The system rolls the die again. The result is a 4.
Step 4 (Player 0): The 4 is added to Player 0's temporary total, bringing their current risk pool to 9 points for this turn. Player 0 chooses to hold. By holding, the 9 temporary points are immediately added to their permanent game score, their temporary total resets to zero, and their turn officially ends.
Step 5 (Player 1): It is Player 1's turn. Player 0 now has a permanent score of 9. Player 1 starts their turn with zero temporary points, meaning their only legal option is to roll the die. Player 1 chooses to roll.
...

## Action String Specification
Player actions: ["roll", "hold"]
  - "roll": roll the die and risk losing the turn total
  - "hold": bank the turn total and end your turn
Chance actions: single digit string for the die outcome
  - e.g. "1", "2", "3", "4", "5", "6"
